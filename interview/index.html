Interview With University Of Toronto Student

Emily: Where do you go to school and what classes are you taking?

Maeve: I go to the University of Toronto and I’m double majoring in BioPsychology and Law. Right now, I’m taking Neurogenetics, Pharmacology, Criminal Law, and an Intro to Python coding course.

E: Why do you want to go into law?

M: I want to go into law because I like the rationality and argument-building that’s involved. I like being able to prove my point and construct an argument that I’m confident in, and I think it’s rewarding to be able to use these skills to change someone’s mind or opinion.

E: Do you feel that you have to be creative in your desired job?

M: I think that there’s some degree of creativity involved, in approaching problems from unexpected angles, but I wouldn’t consider myself a super creative person, so I like the concreteness of the law as something to work with in my job.

E: In what ways does design help you as a student?

M: Design helps me as a student by making some of my work more engaging and interesting, like when I make my notes or a presentation that I want to be easy to read and attention-grabbing.

E: When do you feel the most productive?

M: I feel the most productive when I plan out my days, and have a detailed list of what I need to get done to pressure myself. Even though I’m not really a morning person, I’m the most productive when I plan to get up early and start working. Also, I find that working in a library or somewhere quiet is the most effective place for me.

E: What are some designs in society that you ardmire and why?

M: Some of my favourite designs in society are things like book covers and architecture.

E: Do you benefit from feedback on your work?

M: I like getting feedback on my work, especially in big classes where I don’t get much one-on-one discussion with my professors. Feedback helps me to improve on future assignments, and makes me think more critically about my own work.

E: Will technology benefit you in your future career?

M: Technology will definitely benefit me in my future career, from things like being able to access more research tools and being able to create, edit and distribute my work.
